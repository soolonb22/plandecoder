Privacy & Terms

Last reviewed: 3 July 2026

The short version: Our preparation tools run in your browser. What you type stays on your device. We do not run analytics. We do not sell data. We are independent and not part of the NDIA or the Australian Government.

Your information in the tools

Our preparation tools (the Support Needs Prep Tool, My Fluctuation Patterns, and similar builders) work entirely in your browser.

  • Nothing you type is sent to our servers. We do not have servers that receive it.
  • Your answers are saved in your browser's local storage so you can come back later. Clearing your browser data removes them.
  • You control what leaves your device. Printing, copying or sharing your summary is always your choice.

Payments

Payments are processed by Stripe. Stripe handles your card details under its own privacy policy. We never see or store your card number.

If you buy access, we may know your email address through Stripe or Gumroad receipts. We use it to deliver your access and answer support questions. Nothing else.

The news page

Our news page loads public NDIS news feeds through a third-party feed service. That request comes from your browser. It does not include anything you have typed into our tools.

Cookies and tracking

We do not use advertising trackers or analytics cookies on our tool pages. Local storage is used only to save your progress and your access status on your own device.

What our tools are, and are not

Our tools help you prepare and organise your own information. Every generated statement comes from what you entered.

  • They are not an assessment, diagnosis, or funding prediction.
  • They are not legal, medical, financial or registered disability advocacy advice.
  • All NDIS decisions are made by the NDIA under current legislation.
  • Review any output with a qualified professional before formal use.

Independence

Plan Decoder is an independent resource. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or part of the NDIA, the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission, or the Australian Government.

Refunds and contact

If something is not working or you are not happy, contact us and we will make it right. Consumer guarantees under Australian Consumer Law apply and nothing here limits them.
