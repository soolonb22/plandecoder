Section 1 · Start here

Why this module matters

Choosing who supports you is one of the biggest decisions in your NDIS journey. A good provider or support worker can change your daily life for the better. A poor fit, or someone acting in their own interest instead of yours, can cost you money, trust, and time.

The purpose

To give you a clear, plain-language way to check a provider or worker before you commit, and the words to use if something doesn't feel right along the way.

Why it matters

You hold the right to choose your own providers and change them if they don't work out. Knowing what to check before you sign anything protects your time, your funding, and your safety.

🎯 Learning goal: by the end of this module you will know what to check before choosing a provider, the questions worth asking, and the signs that mean it's time to look elsewhere.
Section 2 · The foundations

What to check before you choose

Registered vs unregistered providers

Some supports can only be delivered by a provider registered with the NDIS Commission. From 1 July 2026, this includes Supported Independent Living (SIL) and NDIS digital platform providers, they must be registered to keep delivering these supports. Providers unregistered for SIL before this date have until 1 October 2026 to complete registration or must stop delivering SIL.

For other supports, you can often choose a registered or unregistered provider. Registered providers are audited against the NDIS Practice Standards. Unregistered providers still must follow the NDIS Code of Conduct, the baseline of safe, respectful, honest conduct applies either way.

Worker screening checks

An NDIS Worker Screening Check is done by your state or territory screening unit. It results in a worker being "cleared" or "excluded" from work with people with disability, and clearances expire after 5 years.

There isn't a blanket rule that every worker must hold a clearance before supporting you, but you have the right to ask for one and to accept only workers who hold a current clearance. You can ask your provider or Support Coordinator to confirm a worker's clearance in the NDIS Worker Screening Database.

The NDIS Code of Conduct baseline

Every provider and worker, registered or not, must act with respect, honesty, and integrity, protect your privacy, and avoid or disclose any conflict of interest. If a provider only recommends their own services without explaining alternatives, that's a conflict of interest they're required to disclose.

✅ Worth confirming before you sign anything: registration status for the specific support (especially SIL), current worker screening clearance if that matters to you, a written service agreement, and a clear complaints process.
Section 3 · Practical tools

Words and questions that work

Here are practical scripts and a red-flag list you can use before and during your first conversations with a provider.

1
Ask about registration. "Are you registered with the NDIS Commission for this support? Can I see your registration or ask you to confirm it?"
2
Ask about the worker. "Does the person supporting me hold a current NDIS Worker Screening clearance? Can this be confirmed?"
3
Ask about cost. "Can you show me how this cost compares to the NDIS Price Guide, and explain any difference?"
4
Ask about alternatives. "Are there other providers or options you'd recommend, not just your own services?"
5
Ask about the agreement. "Can I see a written service agreement before I commit, including how to end it?"
6
Ask about complaints. "What's your process if I'm unhappy with a support, and how do I contact the NDIS Commission if we can't resolve it?"

Red flags worth acting on

  • They only recommend their own company or partner services, without explaining alternatives.
  • They ask for your passwords, login details, or access to your phone or computer, an ethical provider will never ask for this.
  • Pricing seems inconsistent, unexplained, or higher than the NDIS Price Guide without a clear reason.
  • They pressure you to sign quickly, or discourage you from getting a second opinion.
  • They can't or won't confirm registration or worker screening status when asked.
⚠️ If you feel unsafe, or something serious has happened, contact the NDIS Commission on 1800 035 544. In an emergency, call 000.
Section 4 · Real-world scenarios

Choosing professionals in everyday life

01
Only their own services offered

The issue

A provider keeps suggesting their own add-on services whenever you raise a need, without mentioning other options.

What to say / do

"I'd like to understand all my options before deciding, not just what your company offers. Can you point me to alternatives too?"

What to expect

A provider acting in line with the Code of Conduct should be able to answer this comfortably. If they can't or won't, that's worth noting.

02
Asked for login details

The issue

A worker or provider asks for your online account passwords or wants to control your device directly.

What to say / do

"I'm not comfortable sharing my login details. Can we find another way to do this together?"

What to expect

This request itself is a red flag. If it continues, contact the NDIS Commission on 1800 035 544.

03
Price doesn't add up

The issue

An invoice or quote looks higher than expected, and no clear reason is given.

What to say / do

"Can you break this down against the current NDIS Price Guide, and explain any difference?"

What to expect

A legitimate provider can explain their pricing. Unjustified price differences can breach the Code of Conduct.

04
Not sure if they need to be registered

The issue

You're arranging Supported Independent Living and aren't sure if the provider needs to be formally registered.

What to say / do

"Are you registered with the NDIS Commission to deliver Supported Independent Living?"

What to expect

From 1 July 2026, SIL providers must be registered, with a transition window to 1 October 2026 for providers who were already delivering SIL. Confirm current status directly, this is an area still settling in as the rule rolls out.

Section 5 · Tear-out reference

Choosing Professionals · One-Page Summary

Before you sign anything, check

Registration status for the support type (especially SIL). Current worker screening clearance, if that matters to you. A written service agreement, including how to exit it. A clear complaints process.

Six questions worth asking

Are you registered for this support? Does my worker hold a current clearance? How does this cost compare to the Price Guide? What alternatives exist? Can I see the service agreement first? What's your complaints process?

Five red flags

Only their own services offered. Requests for passwords or device access. Inconsistent or unexplained pricing. Pressure to sign quickly. Can't confirm registration or screening.
