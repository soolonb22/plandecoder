Module 2

Thriving Kids Transition Toolkit

Navigate early childhood transitions from NDIS to mainstream supports while protecting therapy continuity.

🔧 3 tools📄 5 templates
ℹ️ Thriving Kids replaces early childhood NDIS support for many children under 9 from 2026. Follow this 5-step pathway.

🔧 Tool 6 — Thriving Kids Transition Pathway

1

Determine if Thriving Kids applies

Check child's age (under 9), current NDIS status, and disability type. Children with permanent and significant disability may still access NDIS directly.

2

Request a transition meeting with your Early Childhood Partner

Ask for a written transition plan at least 3 months before any changes. Document all conversations in writing.

3

Map all current therapies and services

List provider, frequency, goals, and what will happen post-transition. Use the Continuity Plan Builder in the next tab.

4

Connect school and GP before transition

Provide a School Handover Template and GP communication sheet so support doesn't fall through the gaps.

✓

Submit formal review if dissatisfied

Use Module 4 advocacy scripts to request an internal review or ART external review if the transition decision seems wrong.

🔧 Tool 7 — Therapy Continuity Plan Builder

Map each current therapy and plan what happens after the NDIS transition.

🔧 Tool 8 — Referral Scripts

Requesting a transition meeting

"Hi, I'm calling because my child [name] is currently accessing early childhood NDIS supports. I understand there are changes under the Thriving Kids pathway. I'd like to request a formal transition planning meeting in writing so we can map what supports will continue and ensure there are no gaps."

Talking to your child's school

"We're transitioning away from NDIS early childhood supports. [Child's name] currently receives OT/speech/etc. Can we schedule a meeting to go through the School Handover Template? I also want to discuss what adjustments the school can provide under the Disability Standards for Education 2005."

GP referral for Medicare plans

"I need to discuss a GP Management Plan and Team Care Arrangement for [child's name]. [He/She/They] has been accessing therapy through the NDIS but we're transitioning to Thriving Kids. I'd like to access Medicare-funded allied health to continue OT and psychology."

🔧 Tool 14 — Advocacy Scripts

Explaining "good days vs bad days"

"I want to explain something important about [name]'s disability. The NDIS uses the term 'episodic' — meaning the impairment fluctuates. On good days, [he/she/they] can appear to function well. But this is misleading. The supports required are needed consistently because the bad days are unpredictable and severe, and without supports in place, the person cannot participate in daily life or community safely."

Requesting support for psychosocial disability

"[Name]'s mental health condition results in a permanent and significant psychosocial disability. While medication and therapy help manage symptoms, they do not eliminate the functional impact. The NDIS is not funding treatment — it's funding the supports [name] needs to participate in daily life and the community, which would otherwise be impossible."

Requesting an internal review

"I'm requesting an internal review of [name]'s plan decision under section 100 of the NDIS Act. The plan does not adequately reflect [his/her/their] support needs. I'm attaching an updated functional capacity assessment, a daily function log covering [time period], and a statement from [clinician] confirming ongoing and enduring impairment."

🔧 Tool 23 — Communication Scripts

Requesting school adjustments under the DDA

"Under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992 and the Disability Standards for Education 2005, [name]'s school is required to make reasonable adjustments to ensure [he/she/they] can participate in education on the same basis as other students. I'm requesting a meeting to discuss what adjustments the school will put in place, and to share strategies that have been effective in therapeutic settings."

Requesting a GP care plan for allied health

"I'd like to discuss a GP Management Plan and Team Care Arrangement for [name]. [He/She/They] has a chronic condition affecting daily functioning and would benefit from Medicare-funded allied health including OT and psychology. Can we complete the relevant forms today?"

Connecting with community supports

"We're looking for community supports that don't require NDIS funding — inclusive sports programs, social groups, or community activities [name] could access as a community member. Can you help us identify what's available locally that would be appropriate for someone with [name]'s needs?"

🔧 Tool 23 — Rights & 2026 Reforms Explainer

Your rights as an NDIS participant

✓ Right to choose your own providers
✓ Right to a plan that reflects your actual support needs
✓ Right to request an internal review of any NDIA decision
✓ Right to take unresolved disputes to the Administrative Review Tribunal (ART)
✓ Right to a support person, advocate, or interpreter at meetings
✓ Right to access your own NDIS records
✓ Right to make a complaint to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission

Key 2026 reforms — plain language

Needs assessment replaces functional capacity assessment

A new structured tool will determine what supports you need. Allied health evidence remains critical.

Thriving Kids changes early childhood access

Children under 9 may be redirected to Thriving Kids mainstream supports. See Module 2.

Support lists clarify what NDIS will and won't fund

New "support lists" define NDIS-funded supports. Everyday items and health costs remain excluded.

Provider registration requirements tighten

More providers will need formal registration. This may affect choice and control for participants.
